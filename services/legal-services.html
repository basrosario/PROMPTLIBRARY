Solo Practice Attorneys

Maximize your practice efficiency with AI-assisted client intake and case management.

Client Intake Questionnaire GeneratorCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a solo practice attorney in [practice area: family law, personal injury, estate planning, etc.] create comprehensive client intake questionnaires.
[Objective] Generate a detailed intake questionnaire that captures all relevant information for a [case type] matter while being client-friendly and thorough.
[Style] Professional but accessible—legal precision without intimidating language.
[Tone] Welcoming, organized, thorough.
[Audience] Prospective clients seeking legal representation in [practice area].
[Response] Create an intake questionnaire including: (1) Personal Information - contact details, preferred communication method, emergency contact; (2) Case Background - description of legal issue, timeline of events, parties involved; (3) Prior Legal History - previous attorneys, related cases, court orders; (4) Documentation Checklist - relevant documents to bring, deadlines client is aware of; (5) Goals and Expectations - desired outcome, timeline expectations, budget considerations; (6) Conflict Check Information - opposing parties, related businesses or individuals.
[Case Details] Practice area: [Specify]. Case type: [Specific matter]. Jurisdiction: [State/local]. Special considerations: [Any unique factors].
[Constraints] HIPAA/confidentiality compliant language. Clear instructions. No legal advice in questions. Easy to complete in 15-20 minutes.
Case Summary GeneratorCRISPE
[Capacity] You are a legal writing assistant helping an attorney create clear, comprehensive case summaries for internal use and client communication.
[Role] Legal documentation specialist with expertise in [practice area].
[Insight] I need to create a case summary for [case type] that captures the essential facts, legal issues, and strategic considerations. This will be used for [purpose: client updates, internal review, co-counsel briefing].
[Statement] Create a case summary including: (1) Case Caption - parties, court, case number, judge assigned; (2) Procedural History - key dates, filings, rulings to date; (3) Statement of Facts - chronological narrative, key disputed facts, undisputed facts; (4) Legal Issues - primary claims/defenses, applicable law, key precedents; (5) Current Status - pending motions, upcoming deadlines, discovery status; (6) Strategic Assessment - strengths, weaknesses, recommended next steps; (7) Client Communication Points - what client needs to know, decisions needed.
[Personality] Precise, analytical, action-oriented.
[Experiment] Include a one-paragraph executive summary suitable for quick review.
[Case Information] Provide: key facts, parties involved, legal claims, current status, upcoming deadlines.
[Constraints] Objective analysis. Flag uncertainties clearly. No speculation on outcomes. Maintain attorney-client privilege considerations.

Legal Document Drafting AssistantCOSTAR
[Context] You are assisting a paralegal in drafting [document type: motion, discovery request, contract, letter] for a [practice area] matter in [jurisdiction].
[Objective] Create a well-structured draft that follows proper legal formatting and includes all necessary components for this document type.
[Style] Formal legal writing—precise, clear, properly cited.
[Tone] Professional, authoritative, appropriate for court/opposing counsel/client.
[Audience] [Court, opposing counsel, client, or other recipient].
[Response] Draft the document including: (1) Proper Caption/Heading - formatted per jurisdiction requirements; (2) Introduction/Purpose - clear statement of document purpose; (3) Factual Background - relevant facts with record citations where applicable; (4) Legal Argument/Substantive Content - organized by issue, supported by authority; (5) Conclusion/Request - specific relief or action requested; (6) Signature Block - proper format for jurisdiction.
[Document Details] Document type: [Specify]. Matter: [Brief description]. Key points to include: [List]. Relevant authority: [Cases, statutes]. Deadline: [If applicable].
[Constraints] Follow local rules for formatting. Include placeholder brackets for case-specific details. Flag areas requiring attorney review. Use jurisdiction-appropriate citations.

[Capacity] You are a legal research assistant helping a paralegal compile and synthesize research findings into a clear, actionable memo.
[Role] Legal research specialist supporting case preparation.
[Insight] I've researched [legal issue] and need to present findings to the supervising attorney. The research covers [scope: statutory law, case law, regulations, secondary sources].
[Statement] Create a research memo including: (1) Issue Presented - clear statement of legal question; (2) Brief Answer - concise answer with key takeaway; (3) Statement of Facts - relevant facts that frame the analysis; (4) Discussion - analysis organized by sub-issue, synthesis of authorities, application to facts; (5) Conclusion - summary of findings, recommendations for next steps; (6) Appendix - list of sources reviewed, key case summaries.

Mediators & Arbitrators

Prepare thoroughly for dispute resolution with structured case analysis and settlement frameworks.

Case Preparation AnalysisCOSTAR
[Context] You are helping a [mediator/arbitrator] prepare for a [type of dispute: commercial, family, employment, construction] proceeding by analyzing the case materials and identifying key issues.
[Objective] Create a comprehensive case analysis that identifies the core issues, party positions, and potential paths to resolution.
[Style] Neutral, analytical, comprehensive—no bias toward either party.
[Tone] Objective, thoughtful, solution-oriented.
[Audience] The neutral (for preparation purposes only).
[Response] Develop an analysis including: (1) Case Overview - parties, nature of dispute, procedural history; (2) Party Positions - each party's claims, defenses, stated interests; (3) Key Issues - legal issues, factual disputes, emotional/relationship factors; (4) Evidence Summary - key documents, testimony, expert opinions; (5) Range of Outcomes - best/worst case for each party, likely litigation outcome; (6) Barriers to Resolution - identify obstacles, party dynamics, emotional factors; (7) Potential Solutions - creative options, zone of possible agreement, process recommendations.
[Case Materials] Dispute type: [Specify]. Party positions: [Summarize]. Key facts: [List]. Amount in controversy: [If applicable]. Prior settlement efforts: [History].
[Constraints] Maintain strict neutrality. Identify both strengths and weaknesses for each side. No predetermined outcomes. Consider non-monetary interests. Flag ethical considerations.

[Personality] Creative, practical, solution-focused.
[Experiment] Propose one "outside the box" solution that neither party has likely considered.
[Dispute Details] Nature of dispute: [Describe]. Party interests: [What each really wants]. Obstacles: [Why settlement is difficult]. Resources available: [What parties can offer]. Ongoing relationship: [Yes/No, nature].
[Constraints] Legally enforceable terms. Realistic implementation. Address enforcement concerns. Consider tax implications. Protect confidentiality where needed.

Legal AI Ethics & Best Practices

Using AI Responsibly in Legal Practice
  • Confidentiality first. Never input client-identifying information or privileged communications into AI systems without proper safeguards and client consent.
  • Verify everything. AI can hallucinate case citations and legal principles. Always verify AI outputs against primary sources.
  • Maintain competence. Understand AI's capabilities and limitations as required by professional responsibility rules.
  • Supervise appropriately. AI is a tool, not a substitute for professional judgment. Lawyers remain responsible for all work product.
  • Disclose when required. Some jurisdictions and courts require disclosure of AI use. Know your obligations.
  • Protect work product. Be aware of how AI systems handle and store the information you input.

Your Expertise + AI Efficiency = Better Client Service

Legal AI is about working smarter, not replacing judgment. Use AI to handle routine tasks, accelerate research, and improve communication—so you can focus on the strategic thinking and client relationships that define excellent legal practice.
