Critical Knowledge

AI Is Powerful, But Not Infallible

Artificial Intelligence can transform how we work and create, but it comes with real risks. Understanding these risks is the first step to using AI responsibly. This guide provides research-backed strategies from NIST, Stanford HAI, and MIT to help you identify, mitigate, and prevent AI-related errors.

Understanding AI Risks

Research-identified risks every AI user should understand

Hallucinations

AI generates convincing but entirely fictional information, including fake citations, invented statistics, and fabricated facts. Hallucination rates vary by task complexity and model.

High Frequency Risk

Bias & Fairness

AI systems reflect and amplify biases in training data. These biases may be cultural, gender, racial, or socioeconomic, often appearing in subtle, hard-to-detect ways.

Systemic Issue

Data Exposure

Information shared with AI may be stored, analyzed, or used for training. Privacy risks increase dramatically when users share sensitive personal or organizational data.

Privacy Risk

Over-Reliance

Automation bias leads users to accept AI outputs without critical evaluation. This erodes human skills and judgment over time, creating unhealthy dependency.

Behavioral Risk

The VERIFY Framework

A research-backed approach to evaluating AI outputs

V

Validate Sources

Check if cited sources actually exist and say what the AI claims. Hallucinated citations are surprisingly common even in sophisticated models.

Example Action

"Search for the exact citation in Google Scholar or the journal's website. If unfindable, it likely doesn't exist."

E

Examine Logic

Analyze whether the reasoning makes sense. Look for logical gaps, unsupported leaps, circular arguments, or conclusions that don't follow from premises.

Example Action

"Ask yourself: Does each step follow logically from the previous? Are there assumptions being made without evidence?"

R

Research Further

Cross-reference important claims with reliable independent sources. One source isn't enough for critical information. Triangulate facts from multiple authorities.

Example Action

"Find at least two independent, authoritative sources that confirm the key facts before relying on them."

I

Inspect for Recency

AI training data has cutoff dates. Information may be outdated for fast-changing topics like technology, policy, current events, or scientific research.

Example Action

"Check the model's knowledge cutoff date. For recent topics, verify with current official sources."

F

Flag Bias

Consider if the response shows cultural, political, gender, or other biases that could skew accuracy or fairness. AI reflects patterns in its training data.

Example Action

"Ask: Does this assume a particular viewpoint? Would someone from a different background see this differently?"

Y

Your Judgment

Apply your own expertise, domain knowledge, and common sense. You are the final quality control. Never outsource your critical thinking to AI entirely.

Example Action

"Trust your instincts. If something feels wrong or too good to be true, investigate further."

Real-World Scenarios

Learn from common AI safety situations

Scenario 1: The Confident Citation
Situation

You ask AI to provide research supporting a business proposal. It returns three academic citations with authors, journals, and publication years that sound authoritative and credible.

The Risk

Two of the three citations are completely fabricated. The journal names exist, but those specific papers don't. The AI hallucinated convincing-sounding academic references that could damage your credibility.

Safe Practice

Before using any citation, search for it directly in Google Scholar or the journal's website. If you can't find the exact paper with matching authors and dates, it likely doesn't exist.

Scenario 2: The Biased Recommendation
Situation

You ask AI to help write job requirements for a technical role. The output consistently uses masculine pronouns and emphasizes "aggressive" and "dominant" leadership traits.

The Risk

This language can discourage diverse candidates from applying and perpetuate workplace homogeneity. The bias reflects historical patterns in training data that AI has learned to reproduce.

Safe Practice

Review AI-generated professional content for gendered language, cultural assumptions, and unnecessarily exclusionary requirements. Use tools like the Bias Radar to catch subtle issues.

Scenario 3: The Privacy Leak
Situation

An employee pastes a customer complaint email into AI to draft a response. The email contains the customer's full name, address, phone number, and account details.

The Risk

Personal information has been shared with a third-party AI system. Depending on the provider's terms, this data might be stored, analyzed, or used for training, violating privacy regulations.

Safe Practice

Remove or redact personal identifiers before sharing content with AI. Use placeholders like [CUSTOMER_NAME] and [ACCOUNT_NUMBER] to protect privacy while still getting useful assistance.

High-Stakes Domains

Areas requiring extra caution and human expertise

Critical Principle In high-stakes domains, AI should inform human decision-making, never replace it. The consequences of AI errors in these areas can be severe and irreversible.

Medical & Health

AI is not a doctor. Never make health decisions based solely on AI. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for diagnosis, treatment, and medical advice.

Consult Professionals

Legal Matters

AI cannot provide legal advice. Laws vary by jurisdiction and individual situation. Consult licensed attorneys for legal guidance and representation.

Seek Legal Counsel

Financial Decisions

AI shouldn't make investment or major financial decisions. Consult qualified financial advisors for personalized guidance based on your situation.

Financial Advisor Required

Safety-Critical Systems

Decisions affecting physical safety, infrastructure, or life-and-death situations require human expertise and accountability. AI should only assist, never decide.

Human Oversight Required

Daily Safety Practices

Habits that reduce AI-related risks

Verify Before Trusting

Hallucinations are common and convincing. Cross-reference facts and check that citations actually exist before relying on AI-generated information.

Prevention

Review Before Sharing

AI outputs may contain errors, bias, or inappropriate content. Always read everything thoroughly before sending, publishing, or acting on it.

Quality Control

Protect Privacy

Data shared may be stored or used for training. Redact personal information and use placeholders instead of real names, IDs, and sensitive details.

Privacy

Maintain Your Skills

Over-reliance erodes judgment and expertise. Practice important skills independently sometimes to maintain your human capabilities.

Skill Retention

Disclose AI Use

Transparency builds trust. Mention AI assistance when appropriate, especially in professional, academic, or creative contexts where authorship matters.

Transparency

Stay Informed

AI capabilities, limitations, and best practices change rapidly. Follow updates from AI providers and reputable research institutions.

Continuous Learning

What to Never Share with AI

Protect yourself and others by keeping this information private

Passwords & Credentials

Never share passwords, API keys, authentication tokens, or login credentials with any AI system.

Personal Identifiers

Keep SSNs, medical IDs, driver's license numbers, and government identifiers away from AI conversations.

Confidential Business Data

Trade secrets, proprietary code, unreleased products, and confidential business strategies should stay private.

Financial Information

Credit card numbers, bank account details, and financial statements should never be shared with AI.

Others' Private Communications

Don't share emails, messages, or conversations from others without their explicit consent.

Medical Records

Health information, diagnoses, prescriptions, and medical history are protected data that shouldn't be shared.

Key Takeaways

Remember these principles for safe AI use

AI augments, never replaces

Use AI to enhance your capabilities, not substitute for your judgment and expertise.

Trust but verify

Always cross-check important facts, citations, and claims against authoritative sources.

Protect privacy

Treat AI conversations as public. Never share sensitive personal or business information.

Use the VERIFY framework

Validate, Examine, Research, Inspect, Flag, and apply Your judgment to every important output.

High-stakes decisions need humans

Medical, legal, financial, and safety-critical decisions require qualified human professionals.
