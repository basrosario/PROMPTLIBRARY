Core Workshop Series

Workshop 1: AI Foundations for Enterprise

Duration: 3 hours | Audience: All employees


Learning Objectives:

  • Recognize appropriate vs. inappropriate use cases
  • Apply basic prompting techniques (CRISP framework)

Workshop 2: Advanced Prompting for Professionals

Deliverables: Personal prompt library (20+ templates), workflow documentation, quality checklist

Implementation Guide

A proven 12-week roadmap for enterprise AI deployment. Each phase builds on the previous, ensuring sustainable adoption and measurable value.

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-3)

Discovery & Assessment
  • Week 1: Stakeholder interviews, current state assessment, policy review
  • Week 2: Use case identification, risk assessment, tool evaluation
  • Week 3: Governance framework development, success metrics definition

Deliverables: Current state report, prioritized use case matrix, draft governance policy, KPI dashboard design

Phase 2: Pilot (Weeks 4-6)

Controlled Deployment
  • Week 4: Select pilot department, train Champions, deploy monitoring
  • Week 5: Run pilot with daily standups, collect feedback, iterate
  • Week 6: Measure results, document lessons learned, refine approach

Deliverables: Pilot results report, refined training materials, updated governance policy, expansion plan

Phase 3: Scale (Weeks 7-10)

Enterprise Rollout
  • Week 7-8: Train remaining departments, deploy Champions network
  • Week 9: Launch company-wide, communication campaign, support desk
  • Week 10: Monitor adoption, address resistance, celebrate wins

Deliverables: All-hands training completion, Champions network active, adoption dashboard, FAQ document

Phase 4: Optimize (Weeks 11-12)

Continuous Improvement
  • Week 11: Measure ROI, gather feedback, identify advanced use cases
  • Week 12: Transition to internal ownership, establish cadence, plan next phase

Deliverables: ROI report, sustainability plan, advanced training roadmap, quarterly review template

AI Governance Framework

Enterprise AI success requires robust governance. Our framework balances innovation enablement with risk management, compliance, and ethical considerations.

Govern, Don't Block

Effective AI governance enables responsible use rather than preventing all use. The goal is guardrails, not barriers.

Governance Pillars

1. Data Protection

Clear rules on what data can be input to AI systems. Classification tiers, handling procedures, and monitoring controls.

2. Use Case Approval

Defined process for evaluating and approving new AI use cases. Risk assessment matrix and escalation paths.

3. Quality Assurance

Standards for verifying AI outputs before use. Review requirements based on risk level and downstream impact.

4. Audit & Monitoring

Logging, reporting, and review mechanisms. Track usage patterns, identify concerns, demonstrate compliance.

Policy Components

Acceptable Use Policy
  • Approved AI tools and platforms
  • Permitted use cases by role/department
  • Prohibited inputs (PII, confidential, trade secrets)
  • Output review requirements
  • Disclosure and attribution standards
  • Violation reporting and consequences
Data Classification Matrix
  • Tier 1 (Green): Public information, general business content — unrestricted AI use
  • Tier 2 (Yellow): Internal business data — AI use with caution, no vendor-hosted tools
  • Tier 3 (Orange): Confidential — Limited AI use, approved enterprise tools only
  • Tier 4 (Red): Restricted (PII, trade secrets, legal privilege) — No AI input permitted
Risk Assessment Framework
  • Use case risk scoring methodology
  • Data sensitivity evaluation
  • Output impact assessment
  • Regulatory compliance check
  • Reputational risk consideration
  • Mitigation requirements by risk level

Governance Team Structure

AI Steering Committee

Executive sponsors who set strategic direction, approve policies, and allocate resources. Meets quarterly.

AI Working Group

Cross-functional team (IT, Legal, Compliance, HR, Business) that develops and maintains governance. Meets bi-weekly.

AI Champions Network

Department representatives who drive adoption, gather feedback, and escalate concerns. Meets monthly.

AI Ethics Advisory

Review board for sensitive use cases, bias concerns, and emerging ethical questions. Meets as needed.
