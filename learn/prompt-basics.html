Prompt Basics

Master the fundamental principles of effective AI prompting. These core concepts apply to every methodology and every AI tool.

Lesson 1

What is a Prompt?

A prompt is the input you give to an AI system to get a response. It's your way of communicating what you want the AI to do.

Think of it like giving instructions to a highly capable assistant who:

  • Has vast knowledge but no context about your specific situation
  • Takes instructions literally and precisely
  • Can produce different outputs based on how you phrase your request
  • Doesn't know what you're thinking unless you tell it
Weak Prompt

"Write about marketing"

Strong Prompt

"Write a 200-word introduction for a blog post about email marketing best practices for small e-commerce businesses. Use a friendly, conversational tone."

Key Takeaway

The quality of your output is directly tied to the quality of your input. Vague prompts produce vague results; specific prompts produce specific results.

Lesson 2

The Anatomy of a Good Prompt

Every effective prompt contains some combination of these elements:

1. Task/Action

What do you want the AI to do? (Write, analyze, explain, create, compare, summarize...)

2. Context

Background information that helps the AI understand your situation.

3. Specifics

Details about format, length, style, audience, or constraints.

4. Examples

Sample inputs/outputs that show what you're looking for.

Complete Prompt Example

Task: "Create a product description"
Context: "for our new wireless earbuds, targeting young professionals"
Specifics: "Keep it under 100 words, emphasize comfort and battery life"
Example: "Similar in style to Apple's product descriptions - clean and benefit-focused"

Lesson 3

Common Prompting Mistakes

1

Being Too Vague

"Help me with my presentation"
"Create an outline for a 10-minute presentation about Q3 sales results for our executive team"
2

Assuming Context

"Make it better"
"Improve this email by making it more concise and adding a clear call-to-action"
3

Overloading

"Write a blog post, create social media posts, draft an email, and make a video script about our new product"
Break into separate prompts, one task at a time
4

No Format Guidance

"Explain machine learning"
"Explain machine learning in 3 paragraphs suitable for a high school student, using everyday analogies"
Lesson 4

The Iteration Mindset

Great prompts are rarely written on the first try. The best prompt engineers use an iterative approach:

1

Start Simple

Begin with a basic prompt to see what the AI produces.

2

Analyze the Output

What's good? What's missing? What's wrong?

3

Refine Your Prompt

Add constraints, context, or examples to address gaps.

4

Repeat

Continue until you get the results you need.

Key Takeaway

Don't expect perfection on the first try. Treat prompting as a conversation, not a one-shot request.

Lesson 5

Putting It All Together

Now you understand the basics. Here's your action plan:

1

Always include a clear action verb - Tell the AI exactly what to do

2

Provide relevant context - Who, what, why, for whom?

3

Specify the format - Length, structure, style, tone

4

Give examples when helpful - Show, don't just tell

5

Iterate and refine - Build on what works
