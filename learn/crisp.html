The CRISP Method

A structured approach for quick, focused AI prompting. Perfect for everyday tasks that need clear, actionable results.

C

Context

Background information about your situation

R

Request

What you want the AI to do

I

Instructions

Specific guidelines for the task

S

Style

Tone, format, and voice

P

Parameters

Constraints like length, format

Why Use CRISP?

CRISP is designed for efficiency and clarity. It works best when you need:

  • Quick, focused outputs for everyday tasks
  • A simple framework you can memorize
  • Consistent results without extensive setup
  • A starting point before learning advanced methods

CRISP vs. Other Methods

CRISP is leaner than COSTAR or CRISPE. Use it when you don't need to specify audience separately or add personality. For more complex needs, consider COSTAR or CRISPE.

C

Context

Context sets the stage. It tells the AI about your situation, background, and relevant details that will shape the response.

Good Context Includes:

  • Who you are: "I'm a marketing manager at a B2B software company"
  • The situation: "We're launching a new feature next month"
  • Relevant background: "Our audience is mid-size businesses in healthcare"
Strong Context

"I'm a freelance web developer creating a portfolio website. I specialize in React and Node.js, and my target clients are small businesses and startups."

R

Request

The request is your core ask. Use a clear action verb and be specific about the deliverable.

Creating

Write, Create, Generate, Design, Draft, Compose

Analyzing

Analyze, Compare, Evaluate, Review, Assess, Examine

Transforming

Summarize, Simplify, Expand, Translate, Rewrite, Convert

Explaining

Explain, Describe, Define, Clarify, Break down, Outline

Clear Request

"Write a project proposal for building a customer feedback portal"

I

Instructions

Instructions are the specific guidelines for how to complete the task. Think of these as the "rules" for the output.

What to Include

"Include a timeline, budget estimate, and risk assessment"

What to Avoid

"Don't use technical jargon or acronyms"

How to Structure

"Organize into three sections: Problem, Solution, Benefits"

Priority/Focus

"Focus primarily on cost savings and ROI"

S

Style

Style defines the voice, tone, and feel of the output.

Formal

Professional, polished, suitable for executives

Conversational

Friendly, approachable, like talking to a colleague

Technical

Precise, detailed, uses domain terminology

Persuasive

Compelling, benefit-focused, action-oriented

Style Specification

"Write in a confident but not aggressive tone. Be direct and use active voice. The style should feel like a trusted advisor, not a salesperson."

P

Parameters

Parameters are the constraints and specifications that bound the output.

Length

"300 words", "2 paragraphs", "one page"

Format

"Bullet points", "numbered list", "table"

Sections

"5 sections with headers", "intro/body/conclusion"

Special Requirements

"Include citations", "add examples", "no images"

Complete CRISP Example

Context

I'm a product manager at a SaaS company. We're preparing for our quarterly business review with leadership.

Request

Create an executive summary of our Q3 product achievements.

Instructions

Include key metrics (user growth, feature adoption, NPS). Highlight the top 3 launches and their business impact. Mention any challenges and how we addressed them.

Style

Professional and confident. Focus on outcomes, not activities. Use data to support claims.

Parameters

One page maximum. Use headers for each section. Include a brief "Looking Ahead" section at the end.
