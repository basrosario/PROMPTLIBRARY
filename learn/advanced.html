Advanced Prompting Techniques

Expert-level strategies for maximizing AI capabilities. Chain-of-thought, few-shot learning, and beyond.

1

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting encourages the AI to break down complex reasoning into explicit steps. This dramatically improves performance on tasks requiring logic, math, or multi-step analysis.

How to Trigger CoT

Simple Trigger

Add "Let's think step by step" or "Walk through this step by step" to your prompt.

Structured Trigger

"Before giving your final answer, show your reasoning process. Consider each factor, then conclude."

Without CoT

"What's 15% of 847?"
Answer: 127.05

With CoT

"What's 15% of 847? Think through this step by step."
Answer: To find 15% of 847: First, 10% of 847 = 84.7. Then, 5% = half of that = 42.35. So 15% = 84.7 + 42.35 = 127.05

When to Use CoT

Use Chain-of-Thought for math problems, logical reasoning, complex analysis, planning, and any task where showing work helps verify correctness.

2

Few-Shot Learning

Few-shot learning means providing examples in your prompt to show the AI exactly what you want. Instead of describing the format, you demonstrate it.

The Power of Examples

Examples work because they implicitly communicate:

  • The exact format you expect
  • The level of detail you want
  • The style and tone to use
  • Edge cases and how to handle them
Few-Shot Example

Task: Convert these feature descriptions into user-friendly bullet points.

Example 1:
Input: "256-bit AES encryption for data at rest and in transit"
Output: "Bank-level security protects your data everywhere"

Example 2:
Input: "OAuth 2.0 integration with SAML support"
Output: "Single sign-on with your existing company login"

Now convert: "Real-time WebSocket connections with automatic reconnection"

Best Practices

Use 2-5 Examples

More examples help with complex patterns, but diminishing returns set in quickly.

Show Diversity

Include examples that cover different cases the AI might encounter.

Match Real Data

Your examples should be representative of actual inputs the AI will see.

3

Role Prompting

Assigning the AI a specific role or persona changes how it approaches problems. Different roles activate different "modes" of thinking.

The Critic

"Act as a devil's advocate and find weaknesses in this plan"

Use for: Stress-testing ideas

The Expert

"You are a senior data scientist with 15 years of experience"

Use for: Technical depth

The Teacher

"Explain this as if teaching a bright high school student"

Use for: Accessible explanations

The Editor

"Review this text as a strict editor for The New York Times"

Use for: Quality improvement

Role Prompting Limitations

Roles influence style and approach, but don't give the AI knowledge it doesn't have. A "legal expert" role doesn't make the AI a lawyer. Always verify important information.

4

Constrained Output Formats

When you need structured data, explicitly define the output format. This is essential for programmatic use of AI outputs.

JSON Output

{
  "summary": "...",
  "key_points": ["...", "..."],
  "sentiment": "positive|negative|neutral",
  "confidence": 0.0-1.0
}

Structured Text

TITLE: ...
CATEGORY: ...
PRIORITY: High/Medium/Low
ACTION ITEMS:
- Item 1
- Item 2
NEXT STEPS: ...
Format Enforcement Prompt

"Analyze this customer feedback. Return your analysis in exactly this JSON format, with no additional text before or after: {\"sentiment\": \"positive/negative/neutral\", \"main_issue\": \"...\", \"suggested_action\": \"...\", \"urgency\": 1-5}"

5

Self-Consistency Checking

Ask the AI to verify its own work. This catches errors and improves reliability, especially for complex tasks.

Review and Revise

"After generating your response, review it for accuracy and completeness. If you find any errors, correct them."

Multiple Approaches

"Solve this problem using two different methods, then compare answers to verify."

Confidence Assessment

"Rate your confidence in each claim (High/Medium/Low) and explain any uncertainties."

6

Prompt Chaining

Complex tasks work better when broken into a series of prompts, where each step's output feeds into the next.

1

Research

"List all factors to consider for [topic]"

2

Analyze

"Given these factors: [output 1], analyze the implications"

3

Synthesize

"Based on this analysis: [output 2], recommend a course of action"

Benefits of Chaining

  • Each step gets focused attention
  • You can verify intermediate outputs
  • Easier to debug when something goes wrong
  • Can reuse chains for similar tasks

Keep Practicing

Advanced techniques require practice. Start with one technique, master it, then add more to your toolkit.
