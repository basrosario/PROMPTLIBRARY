The COSTAR Method

A comprehensive framework that emphasizes audience awareness and output format. Ideal for professional content creation.

C

Context

Background information

O

Objective

What you want to achieve

S

Style

Writing style or voice

T

Tone

Emotional quality

A

Audience

Who will receive this

R

Response

Format of the output

Why COSTAR?

COSTAR excels when audience matters. Unlike CRISP, which focuses on the task, COSTAR ensures your output is tailored to who will receive it.

Best Used For:

  • Professional communications (emails, reports, presentations)
  • Marketing and sales content
  • Documentation for specific user groups
  • Content that will be published or shared

COSTAR vs. CRISP

CRISP uses "Parameters" for output constraints. COSTAR splits this into "Audience" and "Response" for more nuanced control. Choose COSTAR when the recipient significantly affects how you should write.

C

Context

Provide the background the AI needs to understand your situation. Include relevant details about your business, project, or circumstances.

Strong Context

"Our company is a mid-sized healthcare software provider. We recently launched a patient portal that has been receiving mixed reviews. We need to address concerns while highlighting improvements we've made."

O

Objective

State your goal clearly. What outcome do you want from this content? What action should the reader take?

Inform

"Help readers understand the new policy changes"

Persuade

"Convince stakeholders to approve the budget increase"

Instruct

"Guide users through the onboarding process"

Engage

"Build excitement about our upcoming product launch"

S

Style

Style refers to the writing approach. You can reference known styles or describe the characteristics you want.

Reference Styles

"Write like The Economist" or "In the style of Harvard Business Review"

Characteristic Styles

"Concise and data-driven" or "Narrative and story-based"

T

Tone

Tone is the emotional quality of your writing. It's how your content "feels" to the reader.

Formal Casual
Serious Playful
Reserved Enthusiastic
Authoritative Collaborative
Tone Example

"Use an empathetic and reassuring tone. We're delivering news about service changes, so acknowledge potential concerns while focusing on benefits."

A

Audience

Who will read this? Their characteristics should shape everything about your content.

Knowledge Level

Technical experts vs. general audience

Role/Position

Executives vs. end users vs. developers

Relationship

Existing customers vs. prospects vs. partners

Concerns

What questions or objections might they have?

Audience Definition

"The audience is hospital administrators (non-technical) who are evaluating software purchases. They care about ROI, compliance, and implementation effort. They're skeptical of vendor claims."

R

Response

Specify the format and structure of the output you need.

Format

Email, report, blog post, script, outline

Length

Word count, page count, time to read

Structure

Headers, bullet points, numbered lists

Elements

Include summary, CTA, examples, data

Complete COSTAR Example

Context

We're a B2B accounting software company. We just released a major update that changes how users categorize transactions. Some users have expressed frustration with the learning curve.

Objective

Create an email that acknowledges the transition challenges, highlights the benefits of the new system, and provides resources for learning the new workflow.

Style

Clear and helpful, like a knowledgeable support team member. Use straightforward language without being overly casual.

Tone

Empathetic but confident. Acknowledge the inconvenience while reassuring users that the change will benefit them.

Audience

Small business owners and their bookkeepers who use our software daily. They're busy, value efficiency, and need practical solutions. They're not tech-savvy but are competent professionals.

Response

Email format, 200-250 words. Include a brief opening, 2-3 key benefits of the new system, and links to tutorial videos. End with a clear call-to-action for getting help.
