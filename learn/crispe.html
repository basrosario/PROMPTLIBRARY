The CRISPE Method

An extended version of CRISP that adds Personality and Evaluation for richer, more consistent AI interactions.

From CRISP to CRISPE

CRISPE builds on the CRISP foundation by adding two powerful elements:

Personality

Assigns the AI a specific role, expertise, or persona. This creates more consistent, contextually appropriate responses.

"Act as a senior marketing strategist with 15 years of experience in B2B SaaS"

Evaluation

Defines success criteria so the AI knows how to judge its own output quality.

"A good response will be actionable, specific to my industry, and include at least 3 concrete next steps"

When to Use CRISPE over CRISP

Use CRISPE when you need consistent persona-based responses, are working on complex creative tasks, or want the AI to self-evaluate its output quality.

P

Personality

The Personality component transforms the AI from a generic assistant into a specific expert or role. This affects vocabulary, perspective, and recommendations.

Effective Personality Prompts Include:

Role/Title

"You are a product designer" or "Act as a financial advisor"

Experience Level

"...with 20 years of experience" or "...who specializes in startups"

Perspective

"...who prioritizes user experience over technical complexity"

Communication Style

"...who explains complex concepts using simple analogies"

Strong Personality Prompt

"You are a seasoned startup advisor who has mentored over 50 companies through Series A funding. You're known for giving direct, honest feedback and always grounding advice in real examples. You tend to ask clarifying questions before giving recommendations."

Avoid These Personality Pitfalls

  • Vague roles: "Be an expert" (expert at what?)
  • Contradictory traits: "Be detailed but brief"
  • Unrealistic personas: "Be a superhuman genius"
E

Evaluation

The Evaluation component defines what "success" looks like. It helps the AI assess and improve its own output before presenting it to you.

Types of Evaluation Criteria:

Content Quality

"The response should be factually accurate and include citations where possible"

Actionability

"Each recommendation should be something I can implement this week"

Completeness

"Cover all aspects of the topic without leaving obvious gaps"

Audience Fit

"The explanation should be understandable by someone with no technical background"

Evaluation Criteria Example

"Evaluate your response against these criteria: (1) Is it specific to my industry, not generic advice? (2) Are there concrete examples I can reference? (3) Is the recommended timeline realistic for a small team? (4) Have you addressed potential objections?"

Complete CRISPE Example

Context

I'm launching a new productivity app for remote teams. We're a 5-person startup with limited marketing budget. Our beta has 200 users with strong retention.

Request

Create a go-to-market strategy for our public launch.

Instructions

Focus on organic and low-cost acquisition channels. Include specific tactics for each channel, not just channel names. Address how to leverage our existing beta users.

Style

Strategic but practical. Use frameworks when helpful but prioritize actionable steps over theory.

Personality

You are a growth marketing advisor who has helped 10+ startups go from beta to $1M ARR. You've worked with bootstrapped companies and understand resource constraints. You're known for creative, scrappy tactics that punch above their budget.

Evaluation

A successful strategy will: (1) include at least 5 specific tactics we can start this month, (2) require minimal paid spend, (3) leverage our existing user base, and (4) include measurable milestones for the first 90 days.
